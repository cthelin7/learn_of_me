Does it help you see Him as a real, living person? About this page Many talk about being "Christ-like." What does that mean? What is Jesus Christ really like? Who is Jesus Christ? Many respond to this question with a summary of His life, or a recitation of doctrine. What we sometimes forget is that He is a real, living person - Jesus Christ was crucified for our sins and then rose from the dead. He lives today as a resurrected Being. He has real thoughts, real feelings, and a real personality, right now. We can know Him and have a personal relationship with Him through conversations with Him, following Him, and opening our hearts to His Spirit. In 2017, Russell M. Nelson (President of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints) extended the following invitation:
"Commence [now] to consecrate a portion of your time each week to studying everything Jesus said and did as recorded in the [scriptures]. Study His laws... His doctrine... [and] His words[.] This may seem like a large assignment. But I encourage you to accept it. If you proceed to learn all you can about Jesus Christ, I promise you that your love for Him, and for God’s laws, will grow beyond what you currently imagine. I promise you also that your ability to turn away from sin will increase. Your desire to keep the commandments will soar. You will find yourself better able to walk away from the entertainment and entanglements of those who mock the followers of Jesus Christ. (“Prophets, Leadership, and Divine Law,” Worldwide Devotional for Young Adults, January 8, 2017)
I took that challenge, and have been recording the insights I have found in the scriptures (the Holy Bible, the Book of Mormon, and the Doctrine and Covenants of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints). I wrote down every new thing I learned about Christ - attributes and qualities of His personality, how He sees things, what He finds important, who He is, etc. It has changed my relationship with Jesus Christ. I have been astounded at how much of His character is contained in His words. This page contains these insights, and my hope is that they can help others come to know Him better, too. Even still, these are just words. The real miracle has been coming to know Jesus Christ better through His Spirit and by following His path. The prophetic promise President Nelson made has been true for me: I have come closer to Jesus Christ, feel greater love for Him, and felt His light grow in my life. I have felt His sweet embrace in my darker moments. I have been guided by Him in making critical decisions in my life. Even as I have pushed Him away, I've realized He persistently, unfailingly loves me and refuses to give up on me. I know He lives, I know He speak to us today through His prophet and His Spirit, and I know the holy scriptures are filled with His words. I have had intimate, personal, direct experiences with the Son of God, the Savior Jesus Christ. I "commend you to seek this Jesus of whom the prophets and apostles have written" (Ether 12:41) and encourage you to follow Him and come to know Him for yourself. Statistics Below is a breakdown of how many insights I recorded in each book of scripture. The bars show the number of insights I recorded, while the orange line records the density of observations (insights/number of pages): that is, I may have recorded less insights for one book than another, but it might have been because that book is much shorter. The density is the number of insights recorded divided by the number of pages in that book - that way, if a very short book contains many insights into Christ's character, you can compare it more fairly with really big books. This becomes really apparent in the book of Moroni: it is only 13 pages long, yet it has the highest density of any of the other books. This reveals something interesting - Moroni was the last author in the Book of Mormon. He says he didn't have much room to write in the record passed down to him, but his overwhelmbing desire was to testify of Jesus Christ and "commend you to seek that Jesus." While other authors had the luxury of interspersing their testimony with accounts of history and other events, Moroni devoted every bit of space he had to testifying of Jesus Christ. Old Testament
